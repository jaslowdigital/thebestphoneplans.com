How to Choose the Best Phone Plan in 2025

Written by

Sarah Mitchell, Wireless Expert

With so many carriers, plans, and features available, finding the right phone plan can feel overwhelming. This comprehensive guide will walk you through everything you need to consider when choosing a mobile phone plan in 2025, helping you find the perfect balance of features, coverage, and price.

Understanding Your Needs

Before comparing plans, it's crucial to understand your own usage patterns and requirements. Consider these factors:

1. Data Usage

In 2025, data usage continues to be the most important factor in selecting a plan. Consider how much data you typically use:

  • Light users (0-5GB): If you primarily use WiFi and only occasionally stream video or music when out, a plan with 5GB or less might be sufficient.
  • Average users (5-15GB): If you regularly stream music, use navigation, and occasionally watch videos or use social media apps while away from WiFi.
  • Heavy users (15GB+): If you frequently stream video content, participate in video calls, or use your phone as a hotspot, you'll likely need an unlimited plan.

Check your past few months of usage in your carrier's app to get a clear picture of your data needs.

2. Coverage Requirements

All the data in the world doesn't matter if you can't get a signal. Consider:

  • Where you live: Urban areas typically have good coverage from all carriers, but rural areas can vary significantly.
  • Where you work: If you work in a building with poor reception, certain carriers might perform better than others.
  • Where you travel: If you frequently visit specific locations, check carrier coverage maps specifically for those areas.

Remember that coverage maps from carriers tend to be optimistic. Ask friends or colleagues about their experience with different carriers in your area for more reliable information.

3. Number of Lines

Family plans or multi-line accounts typically offer significant discounts per line. Consider:

  • How many lines your household needs
  • If everyone needs the same amount of data
  • Whether you need parental controls or other family features

4. International Usage

If you travel internationally or call international numbers regularly:

  • Check which countries are covered in international plans
  • Compare the daily/monthly fees for international access
  • Consider if you need data, talk, or both when abroad

Some carriers like Google Fi and T-Mobile offer better international options than others.

Types of Carriers and Plans

Major Carriers vs. MVNOs

There are two main types of wireless providers:

  • Major Network Carriers: Verizon, AT&T, and T-Mobile own and operate their own network infrastructure. They typically offer:
    • The most comprehensive coverage
    • Priority data access during network congestion
    • Better customer service
    • Higher prices
    • Phone financing options and device deals
  • Mobile Virtual Network Operators (MVNOs): Companies like Mint Mobile, Visible, and Cricket use the major carriers' networks but offer their own pricing structures and customer service. They typically offer:
    • Lower prices
    • No contracts
    • Potentially deprioritized data during congestion
    • Fewer perks and extras
    • Limited customer service options

Plan Types

Common plan structures include:

  • Postpaid: Traditional plans where you pay after you use the service, typically with better perks and priority data.
  • Prepaid: You pay in advance for your service, often with no credit check and lower prices but fewer perks.
  • Unlimited plans: Provide unlimited data, but often with fine print about throttling after a certain threshold.
  • Limited data plans: Set data allowances at a lower price point.
  • Pay-as-you-go: You only pay for exactly what you use, ideal for very light or occasional users.

Important Features to Consider

Data Prioritization and Speed

Not all "unlimited" plans provide the same data experience:

  • Premium data: A set amount of high-priority data that won't be slowed down during network congestion
  • Deprioritization threshold: After using a certain amount of data, your speed might be reduced when the network is busy
  • Hard throttling: Some plans reduce speeds dramatically after a certain usage point, regardless of network conditions

Major carriers typically offer more premium data than MVNOs.

Mobile Hotspot

If you need to connect other devices to your phone's internet:

  • Check if mobile hotspot is included or costs extra
  • Note how much hotspot data is provided (often limited even on unlimited plans)
  • Check the hotspot speed limits

5G Access

In 2025, 5G access has become standard, but the quality varies:

  • mmWave 5G: Ultra-fast but limited range
  • Mid-band 5G: Good balance of speed and coverage
  • Low-band 5G: Widespread but only marginally faster than 4G LTE

Check which types of 5G are available in your area and whether your preferred plan includes access to all types.

Perks and Extras

Major carriers often include valuable perks that might offset the higher monthly cost:

  • Streaming service subscriptions (Netflix, Disney+, etc.)
  • Cloud storage
  • International benefits
  • Device protection
  • WiFi calling and texting

Calculate the value of these perks if you'd be paying for them anyway.

  • Mint Mobile 5GB Plan: $15/month (prepaid in 3-month increments)
  • Visible Base Plan: $25/month for unlimited data
  • T-Mobile Connect: $25/month for 6GB of data

Best for Coverage-First Users

  • Verizon Play More Unlimited: Excellent coverage with entertainment perks
  • AT&T Unlimited Extra: Strong nationwide coverage with 50GB premium data
  • US Mobile Premium Plan: Uses Verizon's network with more affordable pricing

Best for Families

  • T-Mobile Magenta MAX Family Plan: Truly unlimited premium data for everyone
  • Visible Party Pay: Each line just $25/month regardless of how many you add
  • Cricket Unlimited Family Plan: Significant discounts for multiple lines

Best for International Travelers

  • Google Fi Unlimited Plus: Free data and texting in over 200 countries
  • T-Mobile Magenta MAX: Free international texting and data (albeit at slower speeds)
  • AT&T International Day Pass: For occasional international travelers

Tips for Switching Carriers

  1. Check your current contract for any early termination fees or device payment balances
  2. Keep your phone number by requesting a transfer (port) to your new carrier
  3. Compare BYOD discounts vs. new phone promotions
  4. Don't cancel your old service before activating the new one
  5. Test before you commit with a prepaid plan on your target network
  6. Look for switching bonuses and promotions, which can be substantial

Final Thoughts

There's no single "best" phone plan—only the best plan for your specific needs. By understanding your usage patterns, coverage requirements, and budget constraints, you can find a plan that offers the right balance of features and value.

Remember that the wireless industry changes rapidly, with new plans and promotions appearing regularly. Don't be afraid to switch carriers if a better option emerges, and consider reevaluating your plan once a year to ensure you're still getting the best value.
